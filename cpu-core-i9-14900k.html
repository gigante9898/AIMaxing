Processor

Intel Core i9-14900K

6 GHz-class hybrid horsepower for prosumers

Intel's Raptor Lake Refresh pushes 8 P-cores to 6 GHz while 16 efficient cores keep background tasks, compiles, and encodes flowing even under heavy creative loads.

Explore other processors

Why it leads right now

With updated microcode and improved binning, the 14900K sustains higher all-core frequencies than its predecessors, making it the best drop-in upgrade for mature Z690 and Z790 systems.

DDR5-7200 memory support and Thunderbolt 4 I/O give creators high-speed ingest pipelines without sacrificing top-tier gaming performance.

Performance highlights

  • Intel Thermal Velocity Boost opportunistically hits 6 GHz on lightly threaded tasks.
  • 16 E-cores manage background rendering, capture, and streaming without harming frame rates.
  • Pair with a premium VRM board to avoid current limiting during all-core workloads.

Key specifications

  • ArchitectureRaptor Lake Refresh
  • Cores / Threads24 / 32 (8P + 16E)
  • Base / Boost3.2 GHz / 6.0 GHz (TVB)
  • Cache36 MB Intel Smart Cache
  • SocketLGA1700
  • Processor Base Power125 W

Best suited for

Hybrid gamers and creators who need peak single-thread speed with heavy multitasking.

Recommended pairings

  • MSI MEG Z790 ACE MAX24+1+2 phase VRM keeps the 14900K cool and stable.
  • Corsair iCUE Link H150i LCD360 mm liquid cooling to tame 250 W workloads.

Buyer checklist

  • Enable Intel Adaptive Boost and watch cooling headroom to hold 5.6 GHz all-core.
  • Use DDR5 kits with robust heatspreaders above 7000 MT/s.
  • Plan for ATX 3.0 power delivery if pairing with flagship GPUs.
